## SEASONAL MONETIZATION STRATEGY

### Fall (August–October): Bulb Content

**Revenue model:** Modest direct affiliate revenue ($25-$30 Year 1, $145+ Year 2). Real value is email list growth, SEO authority compounding, and filling the traffic gap when shrub/tree content goes quiet. The investigative bulb comparison article is the potential breakout ($500-$1,000/year once ranked).

**Estimated metrics:**
- Year 1: ~9,000 fall visitors, ~$25-30 affiliate revenue, ~180 email signups
- Year 2+: ~24,000 fall visitors, ~$145 affiliate revenue, ~480 email signups
- Investigative article (standalone): 5,000-10,000 visits/year, $500-$1,000/year

**Content plan:** 5-8 bulb articles published August, targeting fall planting search traffic. Plus the two-part investigative piece (Part 1 October 2026, Part 2 April 2027).

### Winter (November–February): Planning Season + Preorder Content

**Strategy chosen:** Option C — serve the same outdoor gardener audience during their planning phase. No pivot to houseplants in Year 1.

**Content themes:**
- November–December: Planning season content (12-month yard plans, front yard makeovers, landscape budgeting). Gift content ("Best Plants to Give as Gifts"). Wave 5 articles.
- January: Preorder season content ("What to Order Now Before Spring Sells Out"). Seed starting content (ties into seed price comparison roadmap feature). Wave 6 articles begin.
- February: Bare-root season opens — bare-root roses, fruit trees at lowest annual prices. Publish bare-root buying guides timed to when nurseries start shipping.
- Ongoing: Industry insider articles, evergreen listicles, nursery reviews — all publishable year-round with no seasonal dependency.

**Revenue model:** Lower traffic than spring/fall but nonzero. Preorder and bare-root content catches early-bird buyers locking in spring prices. January-February is when serious gardeners spend. Email list from fall bulb content starts receiving spring prep emails.

**Year 2 consideration:** Revisit indoor plants / houseplant expansion if audience data supports it. Would require PPT to expand tracking to houseplant-specific nurseries (Bloomscape, The Sill, Plants.com). Don't dilute outdoor plant brand identity until the core is established.

### Spring (March–May): Peak Revenue Season

This is where the money is. All fall/winter content, email list growth, and SEO authority feeds into spring purchase traffic. Wave 1 and Wave 2 articles are the revenue engine.

### Summer (June–July): Deep-Dive Content Season

Publish Wave 3 articles. Traffic is moderate — people are gardening, not shopping. Focus on comparison articles and project guides that rank for fall and following spring.
